Context:

Venituri Cercetare (Research Revenue, in thousands of RON — "mii lei") — The total research income received by the university in a given year, expressed in thousands of Romanian Lei (mii lei). Sources include the university's annual rector report ("Raportul anual al rectorului"), budgetary execution reports ("execuția bugetară"), and UEFISCDI/Ministry of Education financial disclosures.

Sources include the Romanian National Institute of Statistics (INS), the Ministry of Education's graduate tracking studies, university career center reports, national employability surveys, and data published by organizations such as the Romanian Agency for Quality Assurance in Higher Education (ARACIS) or Barometrul Calității.

Ranking — The university's position in recognized national and/or international university rankings for each year. Preferred ranking systems include: Metarankingul Universitar (Romania's national metaranking published by the Ministry of Education, based on the classification from Law 1/2011), AD Astra Romanian Research Ranking, Webometrics (Ranking Web of Universities), SCImago Institutions Rankings, QS World University Rankings, Times Higher Education (THE) World University Rankings, U-Multirank, or UniRank.
